Chelsea's History

Chelsea Football Club, nicknamed "The Blues," was founded in 1905 by Henry Augustus Mears at The Rising Sun pub (now The Butcher's Hook). Their home ground is Stamford Bridge, which has been the stadium since the club's inception. Chelsea's identity is strongly associated with their blue kits and the lion rampant emblem, which was introduced in the 1950s. Chelsea is also the only club to have won every competiotion they have participated in. Here is a list of all major trophies won:

  • Champions Leagues - 2 (2012 & 2021)
  • Premier Leagues - 6
  • Club World Cups - 1 (2025, first-ever winners)
  • Intercontinental Cups - 1 (2021, formerly CWC)
  • Europa Leagues - 2 (2013 & 2019)
  • Conference Leagues - 1 (2025)
  • FA Cups - 8

Chelsea's Squad

Chelsea's current squad for the 2025/26 season includes a mix of established players and promising young talents. Key players include Cole Palmer, Marc Cucurella, Moisés Caicedo, and Enzo Fernández. Several new signings have also joined, including Liam Delap, João Pedro, and Jamie Gittens. The squad also features a number of young players like Willian Estevao, Dario Essugo, Josh Achempong, Andrey Santos, and Roméo Lavia.

Stamford Bridge

Stamford Bridge is Chelsea's home ground, located in Fulham, London. It has a seating capacity of 40,341 and is known for its passionate atmosphere during matches. The stadium has undergone several renovations since its opening in 1877.

Club Anthem

Chelsea's club anthem is "Blue is the Colour," which was released in 1972. The song has become synonymous with the club and is sung by fans at matches.

Did You Know?

Chelsea Football Club has a rich history and several interesting facts:

  • Chelsea was the first English club to win all five major UEFA club competitions: the Champions League, Europa League, Cup Winners' Cup, Super Cup, and Conference League.
  • Chelsea was the first club to have a sponsor on their shirts, which was Gulf Air in 1983.
  • The club's nickname, "The Blues," comes from their blue home kit.
  • Chelsea holds the record for the most goals scored in a single Premier League season, with 103 goals in the 2009-10 season.
  • The club's mascot is named Stamford, after their home ground.
  • Chelsea has a strong rivalry with Arsenal, known as the North West London Derby.
